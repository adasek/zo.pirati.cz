Klára Votavová

Digital Future and Digital Hell

Like other crises, the coronavirus pandemic has worked as a catalyst for progress. It has led to the development of many inspiring digital solutions. Among other applications, digital technologies have been used to trace the contacts of coronavirus patients, evaluate the efficiency of existing anti-pandemic measures, diagnose patients, and detect new outbreaks of the virus. However, the quick deployment of these technologies is also associated with risks. For example, could the new digital solutions be misused by governments to increase their control over the population beyond what is necessary to stop the spread of the disease? Will the pandemic accelerate the development of ethically dubious technologies such as facial recognition? Will data generated during the pandemic remain in the hands of a few already powerful companies and states, or will itbe decentralized and shared through open platforms?

The program section Digital Future and Digital Hell is looking for submissions focusing on good and bad examples of the use of digital technologies during the pandemic. We are interested in topics such as:

  • Where is the ethical line for using public surveillance to stop the disease from spreading?
  • What are the examples of ethical and unethical digital solutions deployed during the pandemic? Do they have some attributes in common?
  • How can we ensure that the information and data generated during the pandemic will benefit society as a whole and not only a handful of digital companies?
  • Will the pandemic strengthen the currently dominant digital companies or will it contribute to the advancement of decentralized Internet?
  • What was the role of states in using new digital technologies during the pandemic?
