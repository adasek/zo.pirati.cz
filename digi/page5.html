Veronika Murzynova

Quarantine Economy and Education 

The effects of the pandemic on the world’s economy will undoubtedly be far-reaching, and we will only understand the true extent of the economic crisis in the coming years.
On the one hand, quarantine measures have interfered with the operations of “brick and mortar” shops and services on a massive scale.

On the other hand, the fear of getting infected and having to close stores has forced business owners to look for alternative solutions, and it motivated citizens to shop online. The segment of online sales recorded a sharp increase in the volume of orders, from daily grocery shopping to clothing.

Education has also had to deal with the effects of the quarantine measures: As teaching face to face became impossible and pupils had to stay at home, schools had to undergo a rapid shift to the online environment, which was extremely demanding for both teachers and students.

For the “Quarantine Economy and Education” programme section, we are looking for contributions focused onthe economic effects of the pandemic, changes in consumer and business behaviour, the impact of quarantine measures on education, and examples of best practices in the use of digital technologies in both sectors.
We are looking for topics such as: 

  • Who are the winners and the losers of the pandemic?
  • How can small and medium businesses recover and even benefit from the changes in the consumer behaviour?
  • What does the shift in consumer needs mean for the culture and entertainment industries?
  • How do we ensure that all pupils and students are granted equal access to education in the time of a pandemic?
  • What challenges lie ahead for the new generation of both teachers and pupils, and how can we prepare them for pandemic-like scenarios?
